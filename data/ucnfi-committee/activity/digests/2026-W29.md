- [vigna-g] "SeedSmith: LLM-Driven Seed Synthesis for Directed Fuzzing" — arXiv, 2026-07-09 — OA-1, OA-4
  Co-authored paper introducing an agentic LLM pipeline that replicates a security analyst's workflow to find crash vulnerabilities in software; addresses the two core failure modes of directed fuzzing.

- [vigna-g] "An Exploratory Study of Interpretation Difficulty in Cybercrime-Related Discord Chats" — arXiv, 2026-07-09 — OA-1, OA-4

- [vigna-g] "SherAgent: Scaling Attack Investigation in the Wild via LLM-Empowered Iterative Query-Filter Backtracking" — arXiv, 2026-07-10 — OA-1, OA-4
  Co-authored paper proposing an LLM-driven system to automate provenance-based attack investigation and address dependency explosion in real-world environments.

  Co-authored paper introducing a multi-agent coding framework that generates interpretable directed computation graphs for robot programming, combining agentic AI with classical structure.

- [goldberg-k] "RoboVista: Evaluating Vision Language Models for Diverse Robot Applications" — arXiv, 2026-07-06 — OA-7
  Co-authored paper proposing a modular benchmark (RQA/RoboVista) to evaluate how well VLMs handle multi-embodiment robot reasoning in industrial and agricultural settings.

- [goldberg-k] "WARP-RM: A Warp-Augmented Relative Progress Reward Model for Data Curation" — arXiv, 2026-06-26 — OA-7
  Co-authored preprint presenting a reward model that filters mixed-quality robot teleoperation demonstrations for imitation learning at scale.

- [goldberg-k] "T-Rex: Tactile-Reactive Dexterous Manipulation" — arXiv, 2026-06-15 — OA-7

  Co-authored paper (with Minh Hua) reframing LLM alignment as a cultural phenomenon — the newest expression of "optimization culture" — rather than a purely technical achievement.

This is the highest-profile policy venue any member touched this week. The report's framing — AI raises near-term risk but investment can shift advantage to defenders — is a substantive position that the committee may want to acknowledge or engage.

It's a direct scholarly intervention in debates the committee will encounter when evaluating AI procurement and governance. Given Raley's OA-1 mapping, this is on-profile, but the argument could complicate any committee language that treats alignment progress as straightforwardly technical.

- **[rauschuber-m] — UCSF Health Converge accelerator.** The program documentation confirms Rauschuber's IT organization is the institutional home for an AI accelerator that validates vendor tools inside live clinical settings. This is the most operationally concrete AI deployment activity visible in the week's items and may warrant a briefing to the committee on scope, vendor relationships, and data-governance arrangements given [han-c]'s adjacent portfolio.
