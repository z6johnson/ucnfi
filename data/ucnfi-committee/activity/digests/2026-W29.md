### Publications (papers, preprints)

- [vigna-g] "SeedSmith: LLM-Driven Seed Synthesis for Directed Fuzzing" — arXiv, 2026-07-09 — OA-1, OA-4
  Vigna and co-authors present an agentic LLM pipeline that mimics a security analyst's workflow to synthesize crash-triggering seeds for directed fuzzers; addresses both static-analysis gaps and semantic guidance shortfalls.

- [vigna-g] "An Exploratory Study of Interpretation Difficulty in Cybercrime-Related Discord Chats" — arXiv, 2026-07-09 — OA-1, OA-4
  Benchmarks human vs. LLM interpretation of hard-to-parse cybercrime chat messages under varying context conditions; Vigna is a co-author.

- [goldberg-k] "GaP: A Graph-as-Policy Multi-Agent Self-Learning Harness For Variational Automation Tasks" — arXiv, 2026-07-06 — OA-7
  Introduces a multi-agent coding harness that generates directed computation graphs for robot perception, planning, and control; addresses reliability in commercial and industrial deployments.

- [goldberg-k] "RoboVista: Evaluating Vision Language Models for Diverse Robot Applications" — arXiv, 2026-07-06 — OA-7
  Proposes a modular evaluation framework (RQA) and benchmark (RoboVista) for assessing VLMs across diverse robot embodiments and environments; Goldberg is a co-author.

- [goldberg-k] "WARP-RM: A Warp-Augmented Relative Progress Reward Model for Data Curation" — arXiv, 2026-06-26 — OA-7
  Tackles label noise in imitation learning by defining a relative-progress reward model that handles mixed-quality human demonstrations without costly annotation.

- [goldberg-k] "T-Rex: Tactile-Reactive Dexterous Manipulation" — arXiv, 2026-06-15 — OA-7
  Addresses a gap in Vision-Language-Action models by integrating dynamic tactile signals for agile robotic dexterity; Goldberg is a co-author.

- **[vigna-g] National Academies report takes a clear public position.** The rapid expert consultation argues that near-term AI will elevate cybersecurity risk but that coordinated investment can shift advantage to defenders. This is a substantive policy stance in a high-profile venue (National Academies) that should be reconciled into Vigna's record and may be worth citing in the committee's own communications on AI risk.

- **[khosla-p] Launch of the Institute for Applied Health Intelligence.** A new UCSD entity explicitly organized around AI and health care delivery is now public. Given the committee's OA-8 work and [han-c]'s and [rauschuber-m]'s portfolios, co-chairs may want to confirm whether there is coordination underway or whether this surfaces a gap between campus-level and system-level health AI activity.

- **[khosla-p] Faculty AI Symposium quote on pedagogy.** Khosla's framing — that AI should "expand student learning rather than limit it" — is a directional stance on AI in teaching that sits alongside, but is not the same as, the committee's stated positions. Worth checking for alignment with [moe-r]'s and [tassio-m]'s work before the next enrichment pass.

- **[palazoglu-a] Admissions review statement carries an AI subtext that isn't fully surfaced.** Press coverage attributes part of the admissions review urgency to AI-assisted essays, but Palazoglu's formal statement does not name AI. If that connection is accurate, it represents an unacknowledged policy development directly relevant to the committee's OA-3 and OA-6 work; if not, the framing in the matched items is overstated and should be corrected in the record.

- **[goldberg-k] Four publications in a single week.** The volume is notable; two of the arXiv items (WARP-RM, T-Rex) carry earlier submission dates, suggesting a cluster of revisions landing simultaneously. No single item is a flag on its own, but the robotics research output is dense enough that a summary for co-chairs may be useful ahead of any public-facing committee communications on AI research capacity within UC.
