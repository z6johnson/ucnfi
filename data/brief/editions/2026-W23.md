## item-1 — President Trump signs EO 14409 and NSPM-11, directing CISA AI cybersecurity mandates and academia-government AI security partnerships within 90–120 days.

### What happened
On June 2, 2026, President Trump signed Executive Order 14409, directing CISA to issue Binding Operational Directives on AI-enabled cyber defense for federal systems and establishing a voluntary AI cybersecurity clearinghouse with industry. On June 5, 2026, NSPM-11 directed the national security enterprise to revise procurement processes for frontier AI models within 120 days, mandates new AI governance policy within 90 days, and explicitly requires academia–government AI security partnerships.

### Why it matters to UC
UC operates federally funded research infrastructure at LBNL, LLNL, and LANL, and holds extensive DoD and intelligence-community research partnerships. The baseline confirms UC has a systemwide IS-3 security reference and P3/P4 data classification embedded in its AI risk framework, but has no systemwide enterprise AI security posture that addresses CISA Binding Operational Directives or the academia-partnership obligations NSPM-11 now mandates. The three national labs each operate under DOE/NNSA governance rather than UC AI frameworks, meaning compliance pathways are fragmented across entities with no UC-level coordination mechanism on record.

### For the committee
Does UC have a designated lead — at UCOP or across the national labs — to track CISA's forthcoming Binding Operational Directives and the NSPM-11 academia-partnership requirement, and if not, who should own that within the next 90 days?

The OGC AI Alert prohibits sharing personal information with AI tools without a fully executed agreement, but the baseline records no enforcement or monitoring mechanism.
