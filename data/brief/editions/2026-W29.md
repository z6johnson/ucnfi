## item-1 — DHS final rule converts F, J, and I visa status from 'duration of status' to fixed admission periods, effective immediately

### What happened
A Department of Homeland Security final rule published July 17, 2026 eliminates 'duration of status' for F (academic student), J (exchange visitor), and I (foreign media) nonimmigrant classifications, replacing it with fixed admission periods and new extension-of-stay procedures. The rule adds compliance burdens and potential status vulnerabilities for international students and scholars at U.S. universities. The rule is effective upon publication.

### Why it matters to UC
UC has no systemwide policy position on federal visa rule changes affecting international student enrollment and research continuity. The system enrolls tens of thousands of F and J visa holders across all ten campuses; fixed-period admission creates new administrative obligations for international student offices and potential disruptions to multi-year graduate programs and research appointments. UC's existing AI governance frameworks — including the Risk Assessment Guide and OGC AI Alert — do not address the downstream effects of enrollment volatility on AI-dependent administrative systems processing student records.

### For the committee
Does UC have a coordinated systemwide response mechanism for federal immigration rule changes that affect international student continuity, and should the committee flag this to UCOP's Office of General Counsel and campus international student offices as a priority?

## item-5 — National Academies report co-authored by committee member Vigna warns AI will elevate near-term cybersecurity risk across higher education and critical infrastructure

### What happened
The National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine released 'Implications of Recent Advancements in Artificial Intelligence for Cybersecurity' on June 24, 2026, with a public webinar. Committee member Giovanni Vigna (director, NSF ACTION Institute, UC Santa Barbara) contributed to the report and is quoted this week in Lab Manager and Homeland Security Newswire: 'cybersecurity will need to improve rapidly to meet this challenge' and 'with investment and collaboration, AI could facilitate a stronger approach to cybersecurity that may shift the advantage to the defenders.' Both press appearances fall within the July 13–19 window.

### Why it matters to UC
UC's systemwide security baseline references IS-3 and prohibits PII in AI tools, and the Risk Assessment Guide v1.1 flags third-party model data risks. However, the baseline records no systemwide field for an AI-specific cybersecurity threat assessment or response framework beyond data classification rules. The National Academies report — co-shaped by a sitting committee member — establishes that agentic AI systems are reshaping the threat landscape in ways that static data classification policies do not address. UC operates AI platforms at scale (TritonGPT, ZotGPT, River, ChatGPT Enterprise at UCLA and UCSF) without a documented systemwide AI-specific cyber incident response protocol.

### For the committee
Should the committee charter a working group — with Vigna's ACTION Institute as a technical resource — to assess whether UC's IS-3-anchored AI security posture is adequate for the agentic AI threat environment described in the National Academies report?
