## item-1 — UCSD names committee member Rajesh K. Gupta founding dean of new School of Computing, Information and Data Sciences

### What happened
UC San Diego formally launched the School of Computing, Information and Data Sciences (SCIDS) with committee member Rajesh K. Gupta as founding dean. The appointment was announced in July 2025 and surfaced in the committee scan across a dozen outlets in early May 2026 as the school stands up. SCIDS consolidates UCSD's AI, computing, and data-science footprint into a single school-level unit.

### Why it matters to UC
UCSD already runs TritonGPT — the deepest enterprise AI platform in the system — while UC has no systemwide enterprise AI platform and no systemwide implementation owner; each campus deploys its own (TritonGPT, ZotGPT, ChatGPT Enterprise, Versa). A new dedicated school at the campus that already has the platform formalizes UCSD as the system's de facto AI center of gravity. Whether that's leveraged across UC or simply widens by default is now a system-level question.

### For the committee
Decide whether SCIDS is a candidate to host a systemwide AI infrastructure pilot, or whether the system accepts UCSD's center of gravity widening by default.

## item-3 — NNSA Administrator briefed at LLNL on AI agents and stockpile modernization

### What happened
Committee member Rob Neely briefed the NNSA Administrator at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory on stockpile modernization and the role of AI agents and digital twins, alongside the El Capitan exascale system. The visit is on the LLNL record and signals national-security AI work at a UC-affiliated lab moving into formal NNSA leadership view.

### Why it matters to UC
LLNL has an explicit AI safety research program (the 2024 "Safety in AI" report and a recent California-focused AI policy primer that names CA AB 2013 and SB 53 directly) — substantive AI work attached to the UC-affiliated lab network. It does not have a standalone institutional AI governance framework; DOE/NNSA directives govern. The systemwide governance the UC AI Council coordinates does not reach into NNSA-scope work; the labs sit alongside, not under.

### For the committee
