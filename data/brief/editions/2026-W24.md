## item-3 — OpenAI files confidential S-1 with SEC, signaling IPO path and a structural shift in UC's primary GenAI vendor relationship

### What happened
On June 8, 2026, OpenAI confirmed it has submitted a confidential draft S-1 registration statement to the SEC and has not yet determined timing for further action. This initiates the formal process toward a public offering and locks in OpenAI's transition from a capped-profit nonprofit structure to a public-benefit corporation.

### Why it matters to UC
UCLA is the only UC campus with a formal ChatGPT Enterprise deployment, and UCSF is transitioning to ChatGPT Enterprise from its Versa platform. The S-1 filing — which will eventually disclose OpenAI's financials, pricing strategies, and enterprise contract terms publicly — may alter the commercial terms or governance commitments that underpin those campus contracts. UC has no systemwide enterprise AI platform and no systemwide vendor-risk review process for AI: each campus negotiates independently, meaning the IPO's downstream effects on pricing and data terms will arrive campus-by-campus with no coordinated UC monitoring posture.

### For the committee
Should the committee recommend that UCOP establish a systemwide AI vendor-risk monitoring function so that material changes in primary vendors' corporate structure — like an IPO — trigger a coordinated contract and data-governance review across all campuses?
