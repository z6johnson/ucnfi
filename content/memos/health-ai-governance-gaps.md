## Framing

Opportunity Area 8 — 360° Health Intelligence — is the most infrastructure-heavy of the eight OAs. It assumes a level of cross-UC Health governance that the baseline only partially documents. This memo summarizes what the baseline *does* show about health AI governance so the committee can scope OA-8 against reality.

## What the baseline shows

- **There is a systemwide health AI governance body.** The UC Health AI Governance Forum, led by Cora Han (Chief Health Data Officer), meets monthly. A UC Health Data Governance Implementation Task Force Report (June 2024) defines five Responsible Health Data Governance Principles and proposes a Health Data Access Repository (HDAR). [ucop_systemwide]
- **The individual health systems are uneven.** The baseline covers UCSF Health, UCLA Health, UCSD Health, UC Davis Health, and UCI Health as distinct entities, plus a `cross_uc_health` record for shared initiatives. [ucsf_health] [ucla_health] [ucsd_health] [uc_davis_health] [uci_health] [cross_uc_health]
- **Cross-health coordination exists but is not the primary governance layer.** The `cross_uc_health` record exists to capture initiatives that span multiple health systems; most governance still lives at the individual-health-system level. [cross_uc_health]

## Implications for OA-8

1. **Don't bypass Cora Han's forum.** Any OA-8 work on health intelligence should flow through the existing UC Health AI Governance Forum and the Data Governance Task Force framework, not around it. [ucop_systemwide]
2. **HDAR is the natural anchor.** A systemwide health data access repository is already conceptually defined in the 2024 Task Force report. OA-8's "360° intelligence" framing fits on top of that — it does not require inventing a new data-sharing regime.
3. **Fill in per-health-system baselines.** The committee should sponsor enrichment of the per-health-system entries ([ucsf_health], [ucla_health], [ucsd_health], [uc_davis_health], [uci_health]) before drafting OA-8 commitments. Governance gaps cannot be called out responsibly without that coverage.
