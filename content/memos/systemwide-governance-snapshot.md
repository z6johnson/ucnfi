## Why this matters

The UCNFI Steering Committee's Opportunity Area 1 — the "Trusted AI" Standard — is not starting from zero. UCOP has a council, a set of principles, a risk assessment guide, a legal task force, and a transparency report. A credible Trusted AI Standard builds on those assets rather than replaces them.

## The systemwide stack today

- **UC AI Council** — established May 2022, co-chaired by Alexander Bustamante (SVP & Chief Compliance & Audit Officer, UCOP) and Alex Bui (UCLA). Three subcommittees. Sits under UCOP Ethics, Compliance and Audit Services. [ucop_systemwide]
- **UC Responsible AI Principles** — 8 principles adopted October 6, 2021 by President Drake: Appropriateness, Transparency, Accuracy/Reliability/Safety, Fairness, Privacy, Human Values, Shared Benefit, Accountability. [ucop_systemwide]
- **Risk Assessment Guide v1.1 (2025)** — scoped to administrative AI (not research or pedagogy). Two-phase structure organized against the 8 principles. Each campus establishes its own approval process; Chancellor or delegee approval for "highly consequential" use. [ucop_systemwide]
- **UC Legal AI Task Force** — Office of General Counsel, 18 members across UC Legal, practice-areas expertise chart published June 2024. [ucop_systemwide]
- **Transparency Subcommittee Report (June 2024)** — baseline survey returned 264 responses across UC, 131 units using AI, 205 uses described, 57 flagged high-impact. [ucop_systemwide]
- **UC Health AI Governance Forum** — led by Cora Han (Chief Health Data Officer), meeting monthly. [ucop_systemwide]

## Implications for OA-1

1. **The Trusted AI Standard should be layered, not rewritten.** The existing principles are adopted, published, and cited. Anchor the Standard to them and extend — don't retrofit new language that competes with a President-signed document.
2. **Administrative AI has a framework; research and pedagogy do not.** The Risk Assessment Guide explicitly excludes both. That is the real gap the Standard can close. [ucop_systemwide]
3. **Transparency is the lever.** A 264-response survey already exists. A recurring, public transparency report — tied to the Standard — would give the committee something concrete to deliver and measure.

## Open questions

- How does the Standard interact with the campus-level councils that already exist at 8 of 10 campuses? (See the companion memo on campus council coverage.)
