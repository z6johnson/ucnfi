## Framing

A formal campus AI council is the single clearest signal that a UC campus has an accountable home for AI governance. The baseline captures `has_ai_council` on every campus. Eight have one. Two do not. The absence is almost always paired with other gaps — no campus principles, no risk framework — so "no council" functions as a leading indicator of overall maturity.

## What the baseline says

- **UC Irvine — no campus-level council.** Governance is distributed across OVPTL, OIT, and the Office of Research, with an OVPTL AI Advisory Committee covering GenAI in instruction but no campus-wide body equivalent to Berkeley's PAC-AI or Davis's AI Council. [uc_irvine]
- **UC Riverside — no campus-level council.** No dedicated AI task force. Chancellor S. Jack Hu took office July 2025; governance has not yet been reconstituted at the campus level. [uc_riverside]
- **Every other undergraduate-serving campus has one.** UC Berkeley, UC Davis, UCLA, UC Merced, UC San Diego, UC San Francisco, UC Santa Barbara, and UC Santa Cruz all report a formal AI council or equivalent body. [uc_berkeley] [uc_davis] [ucla] [uc_merced] [uc_san_diego] [uc_san_francisco] [uc_santa_barbara] [uc_santa_cruz]
- **UCOP's UC AI Council** is the system-level umbrella and a useful mental model for what "a council" looks like in practice: two co-chairs, three subcommittees, an annual workplan. [ucop_systemwide]

## Recommended next step for the committee

1. Close the gap at the two exceptions. A short, direct ask from UCOP to Chancellor Hu (UCR) and UCI leadership to charter a campus AI council, with a standing seat on the systemwide UC AI Council, would be low-cost and high-signal.
2. Publish a one-page "campus AI council charter template" derived from the UCOP council's governance model so new councils don't re-litigate scope.
3. Report `has_ai_council` coverage as a public committee KPI. It is simple, measurable, and moves.

## Open questions

- Does a "council" at every campus mean the same thing operationally? The baseline flags presence, not authority. A follow-up survey on decision rights would sharpen this.
- How do health systems and national labs fit into this picture? They sit in a different governance lane and should be treated separately.
